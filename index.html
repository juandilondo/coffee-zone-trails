Quindío · Risaralda · Caldas
Where coffee grows between glaciers and cloud forest, the trails are as wild as the land itself.

The Eje Cafetero — Colombia's Coffee Axis — spans the departments of Quindío, Risaralda and Caldas, nestled in the Central and Western Andes. It is a UNESCO World Heritage landscape of rolling coffee farms, colonial towns and extraordinary biodiversity.

Above the plantations, the terrain rises sharply into páramo wetlands and volcanic peaks. These hikes connect both worlds — the cultural warmth of places like Salento and the raw silence of Los Nevados National Park.
